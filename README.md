# WALMART STORE SALES PERFORMANCE & HOLIDAY IMPACT ANALYSIS
The project Analyses weekly sales across 45 Walmart stores factoring in holiday markdown
events, temperature, fuel prices, and unemployment to understand what drives store
performance.
## key Insights
###  Holiday vs nonholiday avg sales
 

The chart shows that average weekly sales during holiday periods ($1.12 million) were noticeably higher than during non-holiday periods ($1.04 million). This indicates that holidays had a positive effect on sales performance, contributing to an average sales increase of approximately 7.84% during holiday weeks.


### Sales performance by stores
The sales analysis revealed substantial differences in performance among the 45 stores. Store 20 recorded the highest total weekly sales ($301.4 million), followed by Store 4 ($299.5 million) and Store 14 ($289.0 million).

 Conversely, Store 33 generated the lowest sales ($37.2 million), followed by Stores 44 and 5. 
The overall sales across all stores totalled approximately $6.74 billion. 
These findings indicate significant variation in store performance, suggesting that location, market size, and customer demand may play important roles in determining sales outcomes.
### Correlation findings
Weekly Sales and Temperature (r = -0.064) 
There is a very weak negative correlation between weekly sales and temperature. 
This suggests that changes in temperature have almost no effect on weekly sales. 
As temperature increases, weekly sales tend to decrease slightly, but the relationship is negligible. 
Weekly Sales and Fuel Price (r = 0.009) 
The correlation is extremely close to zero. 
This indicates that fuel price changes have virtually no linear relationship with weekly sales in the dataset. 
Fuel price is unlikely to be a useful predictor of weekly sales based on this analysis alone. 
Temperature and Fuel Price (r = 0.145) 
There is a very weak positive correlation between temperature and fuel price. 
Higher temperatures are associated with slightly higher fuel prices, but the relationship is weak and may not be practically significant. 
Overall Conclusion
The results indicate that neither temperature nor fuel price has a meaningful linear relationship with weekly sales. All correlation coefficients are close to zero, suggesting that other factors not included in this analysis are likely to have a greater influence on sales performance.
